Elliott's Wonderful World Of Lockdown!

(I mean, it wasn't that bad)

What worked for me

There were a few good subjects during this year's lockdowns (not a single one in particular):

  • PE - we did nothing
  • CED - again, no work, used it to catch up on my other subjects
  • Digital Tech - as it is computer based anyway
  • Social Studies - was just very well organised
  • Science - we managed to complete an assesment on education perfect
  • Languages - the way our classes were organised, they seemed to run themselves

What didn't work for me

There were quite a few more subjects that did not go so well:

  • Health - my teacher seemed to be under the impression we could do group work
  • English - we had various issues around where our work was actually supposed to be submitted
  • Mathematics - mathematics seems to always take up multiple periods due to the work set
  • Enterprise - not really good nor bad

The best teacher in lockdown

Without naming anyone, I definetly enjoyed a certain teacher's class for it's organisation. Organising a class online less than 48 hours after a lockdown is certainly no easy feat. I can safely say not all teachers were up to the task. This teacher was able to give us new information just as well, if not better than in traditional classwork. I am (at the time of writing) about to take an assesment in this class, and feel that (despite the lockdown) I am perfectly ready.
